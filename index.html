CSS Positioning Breakdown

A simple guide to relative, absolute, and fixed positioning.

1. Relative Positioning

The element is positioned relative to its normal document flow. It keeps its original space.

3. Fixed Positioning

The element is positioned relative to the browser window (viewport). It stays in the same place even when you scroll.

4. Sticky Positioning

The element toggles between relative and fixed, depending on the scroll position. It sticks to a specific offset within its container.
